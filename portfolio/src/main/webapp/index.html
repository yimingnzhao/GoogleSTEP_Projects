About Me

I'm Nathan! I was the kid that always wanted to explore something new, like trying to make plasma by microwaving a grape. Now, instead of breaking microwaves, I'm striving to create without the destructive side effects. When the time came for college, I ended up chosing to study computer engineering, where most of my accidental unwanted side effects could be fixed with a 'Ctrl+C' or a 'git revert', but where I still have the opportunity to make objects light up, process outputs of ultrasonic sensors, and just interact with the physical world with the things I build (even if a few LEDs or resistors are sacrificed in the process). In my spare time, I enjoy reading scary stories, pondering different philosophical schools of thought to discover more about myself, and collecting Pokemon in hopes that I one day will complete the Pokedex, even if The Pokemon Company designs more and more Pokemon every few years.

UCSD: TSE

I am a developer for the Triton Software Engineering club at UCSD. The mission of TSE is to provide non-profits with technical solutions. I have personally been on the UCSD Innovation team, that helped develop the interactive Innovation Portal, and the Internaltional Health Collective team, where I contributed to patient data organization and unit conversion of numerical data for a tablet application that will be used by medical professionals in Tijuana.

ECE Researcher

I worked under the guidance of Dr. Siavash Mirarab on measuring the correctness of an error detection tool for multiple sequence alignments of phylogenetic data. Over the course of my research, I introduced specified errors to correct datasets to determine the effectiveness of the tool under different conditions and designed a pipeline to test the algorithm on hundreds of input datasets.

Google CSSI

The summer after my senior year of high school, I studied the basics of web development through Google's Computer Science Summer Institute program. The last week of CSSI was dedicated to developing a standalone application, where I collaborated with two other students to create FreshFridge, a web application that tracks user inputted expiration dates and reminds users via Google Calendar.

Caprisun Pathfinder

A basic autonomous vehicle that detects and navigates to a radio frequency signal designed for Northrop Grumman's challenge at UCSD's H.A.R.D Hack 2020. The car was built using Raspberry Pi computers running python scripts and interacting with the world via multiple hardware modules. Our team of 5 ended up winning first place for Northrop Grumman's challenge.
